Attendance vs Risk

Students with lower attendance are more likely to enter the high-risk category. Regular participation is a strong signal of engagement. Target: Maintain 75%+ attendance.

Backlogs vs Risk

Backlogs are closely linked with higher dropout risk. Clearing failed subjects early can reduce the risk profile significantly. Max target: 0-1 backlog.

Study Hours vs Risk

Consistent study hours usually separate low-risk students from medium and high risk. More daily effort typically improves outcomes. Target: 4-6 hours/day.

Grade Performance

Students with average marks below 60% are at higher risk. Consistent performance above 65% indicates strong academic foundation and lower dropout probability.

Key Observations

  • High risk students tend to have low attendance and more backlogs than the average student.
  • Medium risk students often show inconsistent study hours and may need support to stay on track.
  • Low risk students usually maintain good attendance and steady study habits.
  • Tracking these patterns regularly helps prioritize counselling for students who need it most.
  • Early intervention can prevent students from moving to higher risk categories.
  • Attendance improvement of just 10-15% can significantly reduce dropout risk.

Actionable Recommendations

For High-Risk Students: Immediate counselling, daily attendance monitoring, and academic mentoring required.
For Medium-Risk Students: Bi-weekly check-ins, study schedule creation, and peer group support.
For Low-Risk Students: Maintain current habits and consider becoming peer mentors.
